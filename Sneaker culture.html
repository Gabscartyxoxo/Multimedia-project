Sneaker Culture

Where did the trend come from?

Sneaker culture refers to collecting shoes old and new. Brands include but arent limited to: Puma, Nike, Adidas, Converse, Air Jordans, New Balance, Reebok, Vans, and Timberlands. This trend emerged in the 1970s, originally in sports culture and then went mainstream as a form of cultural expression. Sneaker Head culture would continue to gain popularity after the premiere of 'Air Jordan' shoe line introduced in 1985.

Who Popularized it?

The shoes would be mostly worn by children/teenagers of color which would then prompt them to become sneaker collectors as an indicator of wealth as well as creativity. By the end of the 90s trend things like swap parties and vintage shops would pop up everywhere in the persuit of collectables and deadstock items. Though black youth popularized shoe collecting, Sneaker Head culture would also become really popular in Asian countries.

What does Sneaker Culture look like today?

Sneaker Head culture remains extremely popular today as it's still an integral part of Streetwear. In the last decade, streetwear as a whole has been revolutionized by working with higher designers to create luxury pieces.
