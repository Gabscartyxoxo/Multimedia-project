Logomania

Where did the trend come from?

Logomania also known as Monogram print is still very prevalent in the world of fashion today. Logomania originally stems from the 80's and is/was designed to allude to wealth as well as status. This style also shows itself in knock offs, luxury goods, and fashion's use as a comodity in the 1990s. Lots of brands will shorten their names into intials to print all over clothing and accesories as demonstrated in the image above of the designer Louis Vuitton.

Who Popularized it?

This is another style popularized by African Americans which would them become a staple in our culture. As stated before, Logomania became extremely popular in the 1990s and a lot of that is due to the credit of the growing hip hop culture. Many people believe the King/founder of Logomania was 'Dapper Dan', a pioneer of streetwear. In the 1980s Dapper Dan created his own clothing boutique where he printed big name brands (Gucci, Louis Vuitton, Fendi, Prada) in cutting new edge ways that other brands hadn't yet thought of. He gained a lot of support from major stars like P Diddy, Floyd Mayweather, Jay Z, LL Cool J, Snoop Dogg, and Tupac.

What does Logomania look like today?

Though Logomania isn't nearly as popular as it was in the 1980s and 1990s, it still is worn. Especially in current times of a recession and financial crisis minimalism tends to overtake the catwalk these days.
