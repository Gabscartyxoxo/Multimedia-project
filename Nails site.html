Nails

Where did the trend come from?

Nail decore has been a sign of wealth since as far back as Ancient Egypt indicating that the women who were them did not partake in manual labor.Arcylic nails would emerge in the 1950s after being created by a dentist, Maxwell Lappe. Arylics were quickly picked up by black women as a reflection of femininity and style. Donyale Luna, the first Black woman on the cover of Vogue in 1966, was sporting acrylics.

Who Popularized it?

Black women are heaily responsible for the popularization of Acrylic nails. After being worn by the first black woman on vogue, other celebrities like Donna Summer, Diana Ross, and Flo Jo (pictured) wore long colorful nails.

What do acrylics look like today?

Acrylic nails remain extremely popular among a variety of audiences. Though in the 1970s and 80s, many white people sneered at acrylic nails, disregarding them as a sign of low class, these days men and women of all ages and cultures wear them.
